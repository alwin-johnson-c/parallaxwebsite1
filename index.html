INTRODUCTION

Financial literacy means “A combination of awareness, knowledge, skill, attitude and behaviour necessary to make right financial decision and ultimately to achieve individual finance well-being”. Financial literacy with good personal finance management leads individual to achieve financial independence in their life.

findings

In a country like India, we have close to 80% literacy, but when it comes to financial literacy, we are not that lucky. In 2019, National Centre for Financial Education did a survey which says that only 27% of Indians are financial literate. So, we have a long distance to travel and put a lot of responsibility on spreading the message of financial literacy across the country. Indian population is estimated at 138 Cr people in 2020 according to UN data. Indian population is equivalent to 17.7% of the total world population over 76% of Indian adults lacks basic financial literacy and they don’t understand the most basic and key financial literacy and they don’t understand the most basic and key financial concepts. The survey report says that financial literacy in India has been significantly poor to the rest of the world. In case of stock market, A majority of Indians avoid investing their money in stock market. In fact, out of a population of 1.3 Billion, there are only 18 million (2% of population) invest in equity market. It is similar story for the mutual fund market in India, only 2 cores investors less than 1.5% of population. Comparatively, equities account for just 12.9% of investments in India compared to 26.1% in the rest of the world.
